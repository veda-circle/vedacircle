Somavara Pooja
Somavara (Monday) is auspicious day for Shiva pooja. Every Monday, we perform Rudra Abhishekam followed by Arati and Prasdam.

Pradosham Pooja
Pradhosha pooja is one of the most important poojas performed to the Graceful Lord Shiva. The evening of the trayodasi (thirteenth day of the lunar fortnight) is called Pradhosha. Pradhosha time is especially meant for praying Lord Shiva. Praying in that time will free us from our sins and gives moksha finally (hence the name Pradhosha). Pradosha Pooja with Rudrabhishekam and Shiva Ashtothra Archana is held every Pradosham day from 6:30 pm - 7:30 pm followed by Arati and Prasadam.

Pournami Pooja
Pournami - the day of full moon - is sacred for Hindus. On the evening of Pournami, poojas are performed to Satyanarayana and Arunachala. Pooja will commence at 5.30 pm. Vinayaka Pooja will be followed by Satyanarayana Pooja and Arunachala pradakshinam(Giri Valam) which is done here as circumbulation of the temple with main deity Shiva. Followed by Arati and Prasadam.

Sankatahara Chaturthi Pooja
Sankashti Chaturthi vrat and puja are described in the Bavishyat Purana and Narasimha Purana. Lord Krishna narrated the efficacy of Sankashti Chaturthi to Yudhishtira. Pooja is performed with Ganapathi Homam, Abhishekam, Ayappa Padi paatu & deepam followed by Arati and Prasdam.

Shukla Sashti Pooja
Shukla Shashti is the name for the 6th Tithi (lunar day). It's the 6th Tithi of Shuklapaksha. Lord Karttikeya (Lord of valor) presides over this day. Pooja is performed with Abhishekam and Alankaram to Lord Muruga followed by Arari and Prasadam.

Anusham Pooja
Anusham is the birth star of Kanchi Mahaperiyava. Rudra Abhisheka is performed to Mahaperiyava paduka and vigraham followed by Thotaka astakam, Bhajan, Arati and Prasadam.

Sastha Mandala Pooja

Sastha Mandala Pooja is the worship of Sree Dharma Sastha for the blessing of his divine grace. Lord Sasta, also popularly known as Lord Ayyappa is known to be the offspring of Shiva and Vishnu (as Mohini, in his female form). Sastha having earned a name of Annadhana Prabhu, mass feasts are arranged for this function .VEDA Circle begins the new year with aplomb celebrating with abishekam, archana, bhajans to Lord Ayyappa and prasadam.

Maha Sivaratri Pooja
Maha Shivaratri is celebrated on the Krishna Paksha Chaturdashi of the month of Maasi which falls in February or March. Of the twelve Shivaratris in the year, the Maha Shivarathri is the most holy. On this night abishekam is offered to Lord Shiva throughout the night and he is celebrated by offerings of Bilva leaves.

Sri Rama Navami Pooja
Rama Navami celebrates the birth of Lord Rama. Rama Navami falls in the Shukla Paksha on the Navami, the ninth day of the month of Chaitra in the Hindu calendar and marks the end of the nine-day Vasanta Navaratri celebrations. Rama Navami is celebrated with bhajans, pooja and the distribution of neer mor (buttermilk) and panagam.

Sankara, Ramanuja & Madhva Jayanthis
The life and the work of the three important religious philosophers of our Santhana Dharma by celebrating the day of their Jayanti. Ashotothra Namavallis in praise of the gurus are chanted followed by vedic chanting. In celebrating the Jayantis, the VEDA Circle keeps up our tradition of respecting our gurus.
 

Hanuman Jayanthi
Hanuman Jayanthi is observed either in Chaitra (usually on the day of Chaitra Pournimaa) or in Dhanu (called Margazhi in Tamil). Hanuman is the symbol of strength and energy. Chanting of Hanuman Chalisa, it removes all obstacles in life.

Upakarma, Gayatri Japam and Homam
The learning of the Vedas begins in the month of Aavani with the Upakarma and is temporarily terminated six months later with an Utsarjana ritual, to be restarted in the next Aavani. On this day, a Prayachittam is performed to pardon all the sins committed during the year. Homams are held and Poonal, the sacred thread, is changed on this day. This day is also auspicious as the Brahmins offer libations of water to their ancestors to whom they owe their birth and to the great Rishis to whom they are highly indebted for spiritual knowledge and the Vedas themselves.
The Gayatri mantra is a powerful vedic mantra, the chanting of which bestows upon the chanter many benefits including clarity of intellect. Every year, on the day of Gayatri Japam, the Gayatri mantra is chanted 1008 times, as a prayaschitta (atonement) for faults that one has knowlingly and unknowingly committed. Performing the Gayatri Homam, enhances the power of the mantra to bestow clarity of intellect.  

Krishna Janmashtami Pooja
Krishna Janmashtami, also known Gokulashtami, is festival that celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth avatar of Vishnu. It is observed on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the Krishna Paksha (dark fortnight) in Shraavana maasa and which overlaps with August and September. It is celeberated with abhishekam, archana, arati and prasadam followed by devotional singing through the midnight.

Guru Poornima Pooja
The Full Moon Day in the month of Ashadha (July-August) is known as Guru Purnima.This was the day, when Krishna-Dwaipayana Vyasa was born to sage Parashara and Satyavati, thus this day is also celebrated as Vyasa Purnima. This day is celebrated in honour of the great sage Vyasa, who is seen as one of the greatest gurus in ancient Hindu traditions and a symbol of the Guru-shishya tradition. This day is observed by offering puja to one’s guru and the guru parampara.

Vinayaka Chaturthi Pooja
Vinayaka Chaturthi is celebrated on the fourth day after new moon in the month of aavani. On this day Ganesh idols made of clay are worshipped in all homes. Ganesh is decorated with garlands and worshipped with durva grass (arukampul). Modak, ladoo and other dishes are offered to Ganesha to remove all obstacles and bless the devotee with prosperity.
 

Deepa Pooja & Chandi Homam
The Worship of Goddess Chandi highlights the importance of feminine energy. Chandi Homam is one of the most popular Homas during Navarathri. The core of the ceremony involves a recitation of 700 verses of the Durga Saptashati. With the recitation of each verse, offerings are made in the sacrificial fire invoking the Goddess. The performance of Chandi Homa, frees one of curses, obstacles and blesses them with a lasting health, wealth and prosperity.
